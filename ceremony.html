Parth Gaikwad's Poetry Collection 'Arthakshar' Published

Parth Gaikwad's Poetry Collection 'Arthakshar' Published The 'State-level Literary Conference and Book Publication Ceremony' was arranged by the Kavyasagar Sahitya Manch, Shegaon, and took place at Mathura Lawn during September 15. The Kavyasagar Sahitya Manch President Vinayak Kale, Secretary Adv. Bharat Lahulkar, Treasurer Shriram Wagh, and other members oversaw the ceremony. Prestigious figures such as senior poet Chandrashekhar Pandit, Pushparaj Gawande (Member of Maharashtra State Literature and Cultural Board), businessman Vinayak Bharambe, senior poet Abasaheb Kadu from Amravati, satirist Sanjay Aher from Nashik, Varhadi poetess Sadhanatai Kalbande from Akot, and Dr. Madhavi Gaikwad unveiled the collection, titled Arthakshar. Veteran critic Da. Go. Kale, H.B.P. Dr. Dnyaneshwar Mirge, and Dr. Premchand Pandit also graced the occasion. It is clear that Arthakshar was written by a youthful poet who was driven by a particular passion. In this creative endeavor, 19-year-old Parth Vinod Gaikwad, a third-year engineering student at Vishwakarma University Pune, has encapsulated his experiences and surroundings. There aren't many people who start writing poetry at this age, therefore his efforts should encourage other students. This poetry collection's distinctive feature is its multiplicity of issues, which may make Parth one of the first to examine such a range. Subjects like Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, teachers, relationships, life, the world, objectives, dreams, freedom, and the dualities of happiness and sadness are all vividly brought to life in his poems. Parth's attempt to give words a deeper meaning is reflected in the title, Arthakshar, which is his debut piece of writing. Lalita Puranik, a renowned publisher from Shri Sainath Prakashan in Nagpur who has worked in the publishing field for thirty years, released the collection. Prakash Gayki organized the publication celebration, which was attended by notable poets and writers from Shegaon's literary circles as well as Maharashtra.
